Journal of Information Management

Published 4 times a year, Journal of Information Management Faculty of Information Management journal is a refereed (double-blind peer review) publication (ISSN 2425-9748). Faculty of Information Management publishes the latest research in practice and pedagogical topics that focus on how information Management are used to support organizations or enhance the educational process. To submit a manuscript click here. All manuscripts must be the authors' original, unpublished work. The manuscript must not be under consideration for publication elsewhere. Please refer to the submission guidelines/instructions for authors before submitting your paper. Authors must review the Faculty of Information Management Publication Ethics before submitting their papers. Papers are published online as soon as they are accepted. There are no submission fees or publication fees or page charges.

Submission Timeline

Manuscripts will be reviewed by the Faculty of Information Management Screening Committee which includes the editor and the associate editors within two to three weeks from the time of submission. The corresponding author will then be contacted with news of whether or not the submission will be advanced to the first round of blind reviews (or is being rejected as not suitable for publication in the journal). Typically, the blind review process takes approximately twelve to sixteen weeks. The FIM does not process any submission that does not comply with the requirements found in the submission guidelines.

Third-Party Repositories

No permission from Faculty of Information Management is required for authors to share or post their final Faculty of Information Management accepted and published article in third-party noncommercial repositories, i.e., author's personal website/repository, author's employer's website/repository, arXiv.org, and other noncommercial repositories. Authors must do so after the publication of their article appears in Faculty of Information Management.

Access & Preservation

Journal of Information Management is an open-access journal. Users have the right to read, download, copy, distribute, print, search, or link to the full text of articles in this journal, and to use them for any other lawful purpose.

Journal of Information Management electronically stores backup copies of all published articles in a separate cloud storage for the purpose of preservation of access.
